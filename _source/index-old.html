About

IDETWIN 2026 is an international workshop on Industrial Digital Twins and Emerging Technologies, in conjunction with the 2026 IEEE Consumer Communications & Networking Conference (CCNC 2026), Las Vegas, NV, USA, 9-12 January 2026.

Scope and Motivation

IDETWIN focuses on the growing convergence of Digital Twin (DT) technologies with industrial IoT, edge intelligence, and secure communication systems. It aims to explore how such technologies are being applied to monitor, simulate, and optimize physical processes in real-time across manufacturing, energy, logistics, and other industrial sectors.

Key Technical Topics

  1. Design and implementation of industrial Digital Twins
  2. Integration of IoT and sensor networks in Digital Twin frameworks
  3. Cybersecurity challenges and solutions in Digital Twin & Cyber-Physical systems
  4. Blockchain applications for secure and transparent industrial processes and data exchange
  5. Decentralized and federated learning approaches in industrial settings
  6. Real-time data processing and edge computing for Digital Twins
  7. Case studies on Digital Twin deployment in manufacturing and production lines
  8. Architectures and real-world implementations of Digital Twins for industry
  9. IoT and sensor integration in industrial and mission-critical settings
  10. Gossip learning, federated learning, and edge AI in industrial operations
  11. Data-driven predictive maintenance and process optimization
  12. Digital Twins for supply chain monitoring, resilience, and automation
  13. Digital Twins in energy communities and smart energy systems
  14. Digital Twins in autonomous mobility infrastructures, ranging from urban transport to industrial AGVs, AMRs and smart logistics platforms
  15. Risk assessment and fault diagnosis in smart manufacturing
  16. Use cases from smart factories, energy, logistics, and robotics

Call for Papers

The IDETWIN workshop aims at fostering the development and circulation of new ideas and research directions on building intelligent, adaptive, and resilient urban environments. Topics include edge AI, federated learning, digital twins, optimization, and system-level co-design. The workshop bridges foundational technologies and real-world deployments to foster sustainable, smart, and responsive cities. The IDETWIN workshop solicits contributions on, but not limited to, the following topics:
  • Edge AI architectures and adaptive middleware for low-latency sensing and decision support
  • Federated and privacy-preserving learning on multi-source heterogeneous urban data
  • Mathematical optimization for energy-aware mobility, resource scheduling, and resilient infrastructure
  • Secure, interoperable data exchange via blockchain and urban data spaces
  • Digital twin integration for predictive modeling and incident response
  • System-level co-design for transportation, logistics, emergency services, and sustainability

Submission and Format

IDETWIN 2026 submissions will follow IEEE PerCom guidelines.

All papers must be: at most 6 pages (+1 extra page for purchase only) of technical content, typeset in double-column IEEE format using 10pt fonts on US letter paper, with all fonts embedded. In IDETWIN the peer-review process will be single-blind. Submissions must be made here via EDAS. The IEEE LaTeX and Microsoft Word templates, as well as related information, can be found on the IEEE Computer Society website.

IDETWIN will be held in conjunction with IEEE PerCom 2026. All accepted papers will be included in the Percom workshops proceedings and included and indexed in the IEEEXplore digital library. At least one author will be required to have a full registration at the PerCom conference and present the paper during the workshop (note: remote presentations are not allowed).

Journal Extension Opportunity

The best and tematically relevant papers presented at the IDETWIN 2026 workshop, held in conjunction with IEEE PerCom 2026, will be invited to submit extended versions to a Special Issue of Future Generation Computer Systems (FGCS), Elsevier (Q1, IF > 7), subject to standard peer-review.
